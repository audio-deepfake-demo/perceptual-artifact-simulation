Explainable Audio Deepfake Detection

Anonymous Submission
Paper (PDF)
Abstract

Audio deepfakes pose a growing threat to speaker verification systems and media integrity. While existing detection systems achieve strong performance, they remain opaque black boxes offering no insight into why a sample is flagged as spoofed. In this work, we present an explainable framework for audio deepfake detection that identifies and categorises specific acoustic artefacts left behind by generative models. Our approach maps detections onto an interpretable artefact taxonomy spanning articulation, linguistic/paralinguistic, temporal/prosodic, signal-level, and spectral/voice-quality dimensions, providing human-understandable rationale for each prediction.

Artefact Taxonomy
Five Perceptual Categories

Our taxonomy organises deepfake artefacts into five perceptually grounded categories, each with subcategories describing how synthesised speech deviates from natural human speech.

Spoof
Word boundaries are merged and blended throughout the utterance. The synthesiser fails to produce distinct inter-word silence intervals, causing adjacent phonemes to coalesce in a way uncharacteristic of natural speech.
Poor Word Articulation
Transcript

"to bwock the amendmentw although I would be awfully glad to help get some other kind of amendment ratified."

Spoof
Consonants are mispronounced due to poor articulatory precision — "block" becomes "bwock" and "amendment" is distorted at the final consonant. The model flags anomalous formant transitions at liquid and stop consonants.
Poor Word Articulation
Transcript

"Respiration, that is, the absorption of oxygen and the exhalation of carbonec acidua."

Spoof
Words such as "carbonic acid" are distorted or imprecisely formed, reducing intelligibility despite clear word boundaries. The model detects reduced spectral energy in stop consonant bursts and anomalous vowel formants.
Word / Phrase Repetition
Transcript

"Having determined that Mrs. Payne determined that Mrs. Payne was a responsible and reliable citizen, Hosty interviewed her on November 1."

Spoof
Unintentional repetition of words or phrases detected. The model identifies repeated token sequences in the output alignment absent from the reference transcript — a known failure mode of attention-based TTS decoders.
Mispronounced Words
Transcript

"We stored 2 L at 17°C for testing."

Spoof
Units and abbreviations are mispronounced — "L" and "17°C" are read as raw symbols rather than natural expansions. The model detects a mismatch between expected prosodic patterns for unit abbreviations and the produced phoneme sequence.
Mispronounced Acronyms
Transcript

"Prof Rao discussed U.N. and No.10." (shorthand notations pronounced incorrectly)

Spoof
Shorthand notations like "U.N." and "No.10" are pronounced incorrectly or in an awkward, non-natural manner. The model flags a mismatch between the expected phoneme expansion for acronyms and the produced output.
Unnatural Paralinguistic Sounds
Transcript

"During the physics experiment ...[Umm].. raises additional concerns ...[Uh].. if we want accurate conclusions."

Spoof
Filler sounds "Umm" and "Uh" appear at positions and with acoustic profiles inconsistent with natural disfluency. The model detects non-lexical vocalisations whose spectral characteristics do not match surrounding phonetic context.
Missing Natural Pauses
Overly fast speech — natural breath timing absent
Spoof
Overly fast speech with missing expected natural pauses and subtle breathing moments. The synthesiser produces no breath cues at clause boundaries, making the output sound rushed and robotic.
Lack of Expressiveness
Transcript

"You're quitting tomorrow? You're not serious, are you?!"

Spoof
Despite the surprised, questioning content, the F0 contour is nearly flat. The model flags near-zero F0 variance and absence of pitch rises expected in interrogative and exclamatory utterances.
Lack of Expressiveness
Transcript

"This is unreal! No way! I refuse to believe this."

Spoof
Highly exclamatory content delivered with monotonous intonation. The model detects absence of sharp F0 peaks at "unreal" and "No way", and uniform energy across phrases that should carry strong stress contrast.
Lack of Expressiveness
Emotionally charged sentence delivered flat
Spoof
The emotionally charged and surprised tone of the sentence is delivered in a flat or monotonous manner. The model identifies near-zero F0 dynamics inconsistent with the expected affective prosody for this utterance type.
Awkward Pauses
Transcript

"When the. Final whistle. Blew the. Stadium exploded. Into celebration."

Spoof
Pauses occur at syntactically implausible positions — mid-noun-phrase and mid-clause — disrupting natural prosodic grouping. The model identifies silence at positions inconsistent with natural speech rhythm.
Awkward Pauses
Unnatural breaks disrupt conversational flow
Spoof
Unnatural and frequent breaks disrupt the natural flow of the sentence and affect its conversational rhythm. The model detects silence intervals at syntactically implausible positions throughout the utterance.
Background Noise
Quantization noise
Spoof
Continuous unwanted noise present alongside clear vocals. The model detects stationary noise floor elevation in high-frequency bands, a typical artefact of vocoder quantization.
Background Noise
Crackle sound
Spoof
Crackling noise accompanies the speech signal. The model detects semi-periodic low-energy transients distributed across the signal, inconsistent with clean human speech recordings.
Background Noise
Electric buzz
Spoof
Persistent electrical buzz present in the background. The model identifies a narrow-band tonal component at 50/60 Hz harmonics, characteristic of electrical interference introduced during synthesis or codec processing.
Background Noise
Static noise
Spoof
Noticeable static noise interferes with the clarity of the speech signal. The model detects a broadband noise floor inconsistent with clean studio conditions, flagging it as a signal-level synthesis artefact.
Impulse Noise
Click sound
Spoof
Short, sudden click-like bursts appear at irregular intervals. The model identifies transient energy spikes inconsistent with phoneme boundaries, a hallmark of autoregressive neural codec decoding errors.
Impulse Noise
Thud sound
Spoof
Low-frequency thud-like impulses interrupt the speech signal. The model detects broadband low-frequency transients inconsistent with natural phoneme transitions, indicating synthesis-stage reconstruction failures.
Unnatural Voice Timbre
Metallic and over-sharpened
Spoof
Voice texture is metallic and over-sharpened. Harmonic-to-noise ratio is unnaturally high, and the spectral envelope shows over-regularised harmonics inconsistent with natural human vocal fold vibration.
Unnatural Voice Timbre
Synthetic quality — lacks natural human resonance
Spoof
Distinctly synthetic voice quality that sounds artificial and lacks natural human resonance. The model detects an overly smooth spectral envelope and absence of natural formant bandwidth variation characteristic of real vocal tracts.
Muffled Voice
Muffled / distant voice
Spoof
Voice sounds suppressed, giving the impression of a covered or distant speaker. Significant high-frequency attenuation above 4 kHz detected, consistent with low-pass filtering introduced by certain neural vocoders.
Distorted / Wobbly Voice
Shaky, unstable voice
Spoof
Voice is shaky and unstable throughout. Periodic F0 modulation inconsistent with natural vibrato detected, a characteristic wobble introduced by phase reconstruction errors in GAN-based vocoders.
