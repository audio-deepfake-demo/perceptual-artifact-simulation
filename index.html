ArtiFake: A Taxonomy-Driven Approach for Simulating Perceptual Artifacts in Synthetic Speech

Anonymous Submission
Abstract

Recent advances in explainable audio deepfake detection have highlighted the importance of perceptually meaningful artifact-based explanations that are intuitive for non-technical users. However, developing such systems requires artifact-level annotations, which are typically obtained through human labeling and are costly, subjective, and difficult to scale. In this work, we propose ArtiFake, a taxonomy-driven approach for simulating perceptual artifacts in synthetic speech. We first organize commonly observed artifacts into a structured taxonomy and then design artifact-specific simulation strategies that introduce these artifacts through transcript-level modifications and signal-level transformations. By generating artifacts in a controlled manner, the proposed approach enables scalable creation of artifact-level annotations and provides greater control over artifact categories and distributions. Using the proposed framework, we create ArtiFake, a large-scale corpus comprising over 26k synthetic speech samples covering 13 perceptual artifact categories. The proposed methodology provides a scalable alternative to manual artifact annotation. In addition, it is designed to be extensible to emerging artifact categories, supporting future research in explainable and human-centered audio deepfake analysis.

Artefact Taxonomy
Five Perceptual Categories

Our taxonomy organises deepfake artefacts into five perceptually grounded categories, each with subcategories describing how synthesised speech deviates from natural human speech.

Category Subcategory Description
Articulation-Related Poor Word Articulation Unclear / unintelligible words with clear word boundaries
Slurred Speech Unclear speech with merged / blended word boundaries
Linguistic / Paralinguistic Word/Phrase Repetition Unintentional repetition of words / phrases
Mishandled Non-Standard Words Incorrect handling of abbreviations / date / units / named-entities etc.
Unnatural Paralinguistic Sounds Non-verbal / filler sounds like 'hmm', 'uh' seem artificial and awkward
Temporal / Prosodic Awkward Pauses Unnatural word stress and pauses at awkward positions
Missing Natural Pauses Overly fast speech lacking natural breath timings
Lack of Expressiveness Monotonous speaking style with no expression / emotion
Signal-level Background Noise Constant background noise like static, crackle, quantization noise
Impulse Noise Sudden short-duration noise like clicks, thuds, transient noise etc.
Voice-Quality Muffled Voice Suppressed / distant-sounding voice
Unnatural Voice Timbre Synthetic / mechanical / overly-sharp voice texture
Distorted/Wobbly Voice Stretched / warped / shaky / unstable voice
